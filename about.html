About Me

...and what keeps me driven.

I am an SFU graduate with a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology, having been conferred my degree on June 2018. In addition, I completed 3 full-time work semesters as a Special Education Assistant for the Mediated Learning Academy in Coquitlam, British Columbia.

I am a diligent, practical learner of Full Stack Web Development. Starting in mid-2017, I have honed my experience in a variety of programming languages and frameworks, emphasizing clean code that is readable and maintainable. For creating scalable, data-driven applications, I am a strong advocate of Component Based Architecture and Declarative Programming.

As an undergraduate volunteer, I designed this website using WordPress as a volunteer Web Designer for the Intergroup Relations Social Justice (IRSJ) lab at SFU during undergrad. I also coordinated the template colors and chose the font, providing the overall look and feel for the site, and redesigned the Making Ends Meet logo using Photoshop in the header on the landing page.

I have a formal education in psychology, doing particularly well in the more theoretical/behavioral courses like Personality Theory, Intergroup Relations, Emotion and Motivation, History of Psychology, Learning and Memory, Psychopharmacology, Selected Topics in the History of Theoretical Psychology, (my favorite upper division course where I wrote an awesome paper about neuroscience and free will) and Cognitive Psychology, averaging an A grade in each.
